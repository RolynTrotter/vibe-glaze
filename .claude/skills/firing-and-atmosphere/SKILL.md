# firing-and-atmosphere

The kiln-side companion to `glaze-chemistry`. That skill covers how oxides/colorants behave;
this one covers **how the kiln atmosphere is created and behaves**, with an emphasis on the
user's setup: a **cone-6 electric kiln** with **saggars** used to get **local reduction**.

## Rigor flag (important)

Some of this is directly citable to Digital Fire; some is sound physical-chemistry reasoning
beyond what those pages state explicitly. **Each doc marks which is which.** Never present
reasoning as a citation, and prefer measurement/testing for anything safety- or
result-critical. Combustibles in a sealed-ish kiln involve CO (toxic) and fumes — see safety
notes.

## One-line answers

- **Reduction = an oxygen-starved kiln atmosphere** in which **carbon monoxide (CO)** grabs
  oxygen from metal oxides in the glaze/body (CuO→Cu2O, Fe2O3→FeO), changing colors and melt.
- **In an electric kiln** there's no flame, so reduction must come from **combustibles** —
  inside a **saggar** they consume the local O2 and generate CO/CO2, reducing the ware nearby.
- **Trapped water vapor at cone 6 mostly stays as steam and vents**; it does **not** thermally
  dissociate to any meaningful degree at ~1225 °C, but it can react with hot carbon
  (C + H2O → CO + H2), which actually **boosts** reduction. Details + caveats in `saggar-firing.md`.
